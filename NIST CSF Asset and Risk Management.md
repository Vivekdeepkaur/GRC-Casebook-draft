# NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
## Risk & Asset Management Maturity Assessment

---

## 📄 Executive Summary
This case study evaluates an organization’s cybersecurity maturity in asset management and risk assessment. It identifies key gaps in asset visibility, classification, and governance, and proposes structured controls aligned with the **Identify** function of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF).

---

## 🏢 Scenario

The organization operates multiple IT systems including:
- Cloud applications  
- Internal databases  
- Third-party integrations  

### Current Challenges:
- No centralized IT asset inventory  
- Inconsistent risk assessments across teams  
- Lack of formal classification of critical systems  

This leads to poor visibility and weak risk prioritization.

---

## 🧱 Current State (AS-IS)

- No centralized asset inventory  
- Systems tracked in separate spreadsheets  
- No formal asset classification system  
- Risk assessments performed inconsistently  

---

## ⚠️ Risk Assessment

| Risk ID | Description                         | Impact | Likelihood |
|--------|-------------------------------------|--------|-----------|
| R1     | Unknown critical assets             | High   | High      |
| R2     | Unclassified sensitive systems      | High   | Medium    |
| R3     | Inconsistent risk evaluation        | Medium | High      |
| R4     | Lack of ownership for systems       | High   | Medium    |

---

## 🧠 Control Design

| Control ID | Control Description |
|------------|-------------------|
| C1 | Centralized IT asset inventory (CMDB implementation) |
| C2 | Asset classification policy (Critical / High / Medium / Low) |
| C3 | Defined asset ownership model |
| C4 | Standardized risk assessment methodology |
| C5 | Periodic asset review and validation |

---

## 🔗 NIST CSF Mapping

| Function  | Category              | Controls |
|----------|----------------------|----------|
| Identify | Asset Management     | C1, C2   |
| Identify | Risk Assessment      | C4       |
| Identify | Governance           | C3       |
| Protect  | Access Control Awareness | C2, C5 |

---

## 🧠 Explanation (GRC Perspective)

The **Identify** function of NIST CSF focuses on understanding:
- Organizational assets  
- Systems  
- Data  
- Associated risks  

Without proper asset inventory and classification, organizations cannot effectively prioritize security efforts.

---

## 🧪 Validation Approach

- Review completeness of asset inventory (CMDB validation)  
- Verify asset classification accuracy  
- Check ownership assignment for critical systems  
- Validate risk assessment consistency  

---

## 📊 Maturity Improvement

| Capability Area        | Current State | Future State |
|----------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Asset Visibility      | Low         | High        |
| Risk Consistency      | Low         | High        |
| Governance            | Low         | Medium-High |
| Security Prioritization | Low       | High        |

---

## 🏁 Conclusion

The organization currently lacks visibility into its IT assets and associated risks.

Implementing a structured asset management and risk assessment framework:
- Improves security prioritization  
- Enhances governance  
- Aligns with the **Identify** function of NIST CSF
